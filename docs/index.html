About the blog

Making Sense of Data aims to help companies analyze employee-related data to improve business outcomes using an insight-oriented data approach rooted in the scientific method.

When I started this journey, I had to make several choices. One was to choose the software for analyzing data. Although I was familiar with Excel, I began considering whether to enhance my Excel skills or start learning a statistical program. Ultimately, I decided to learn R, a statistical program. The R community offers many open resources that significantly help my learning process. In addition, I wanted to document and share my work. And Quarto became the perfect tool for me to do this. I was able to create this space where I can share my learning and my work.

This blog is about my journey to make sense of people data. It has two sections: one for storing and sharing projects, notes, and ideas, and another for sharing R and Quarto open resources I’ve gathered along the way. I hope you will find it useful, and feel free to share it with anyone!

About me

Cozmina Secula, Creator of Making Sense of Data blog

With an extensive background in B2B sales and people management, I became interested in using data to answer questions during a master’s program in strategic human resource management at “Alexandru Ioan Cuza” University, Iasi.

After completing a research project with qualitative data, I started working with quantitative data. This led me to initiate Making Sense of Data blog, focusing on people analytics projects. I am also passionate about open-source knowledge.
